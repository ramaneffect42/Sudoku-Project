My Experience in Hackathons

Hackathon 1: HackHorizons by WHHC

I took part in HackHorizons with the goal of creating a practical tool to support students in their academic journey. The result was a dynamic study-buddy web application that goes beyond simple information retrieval. It's designed to act as a personal tutor, allowing a student to pose a question and receive a detailed, well-structured answer. What makes this application particularly effective is its ability to understand the user's educational background and tailor the response accordingly, ensuring the information is always relevant and easy to grasp. To provide a holistic learning experience, the app also intelligently curates and suggests a list of relevant videos, offering an alternative medium for understanding complex topics and fostering a more well-rounded approach to studying.


Tech Stack:

  • HTML
  • CSS
  • Python (Flask)
  • JavaScript
  • SQLite

Hackathon 2: Sustain-ai-thon in VITC

During the Sustain-ai-thon, my teammate and I focused our efforts on tackling a critical environmental issue: water pollution. Our project involved building a web application that gives a voice to concerned citizens, empowering them to contribute to a larger ecological effort. The primary function of the app is ingeniously simple yet powerful: a user can upload a photograph of a polluted water body, and our custom-built model instantly analyzes the image. By examining the color and clarity of the water, the model provides a quantitative assessment of the pollution level. The vision for this tool was to create a community-driven database of environmental issues, with a planned feature to plot these locations on a map, providing valuable, real-time data to government and environmental agencies who could then take swift action.


Tech Stack:

  • HTML
  • CSS
  • Python (Flask)
  • Python (OpenCV)
  • Jinja for Templating (use of a base page)
  • JavaScript
  • SQLite

Hackathon 3: Nexathon in VITC

For Nexathon, our team built a project centered on health and fitness. My core contribution was developing a sophisticated computer vision module that serves as a virtual personal trainer. The application allows users to upload a video of themselves performing exercises or yoga poses. The module then meticulously analyzes the video, using advanced techniques to track and identify key points on the user's body. By comparing the user's form to an ideal standard, the app provides precise, constructive feedback, helping them correct their posture and technique in order to maximize their workout's effectiveness and prevent injury. While our initial ambitions included implementing real-time feedback and a competitive leaderboard, the experience was invaluable, pushing the boundaries of what we could achieve with computer vision and setting a clear roadmap for future improvements.


Tech Stack:

  • HTML
  • CSS
  • Python (Flask)
  • Open CV and MediaPipe
  • Jinja for Templating (use of a base page)
  • JavaScript
  • SQLite
